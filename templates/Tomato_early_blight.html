Tomato Early Blight

Tomato early blight, caused by the fungus Alternaria solani, is a common disease affecting tomato plants. It leads to dark spots on leaves, stems, and fruits, significantly reducing yield and quality.

Symptoms include small, brown lesions with concentric rings that gradually expand. Infected leaves turn yellow and die, weakening the plant. Severe infections can affect stems and fruits, leading to significant losses.

The disease spreads through spores carried by wind, water, and contaminated soil. Favorable conditions include warm, humid weather, making it difficult to control once established.

Effective management includes crop rotation, resistant varieties, and timely application of fungicides.

Medicines

Mancozeb: A protective fungicide commonly used to prevent and control early blight in tomatoes.

Chlorothalonil: A broad-spectrum fungicide effective in controlling fungal infections in tomatoes.

Azoxystrobin: A systemic fungicide providing long-lasting protection against early blight.

Copper Oxychloride: A contact fungicide widely used to control early blight in tomatoes.
