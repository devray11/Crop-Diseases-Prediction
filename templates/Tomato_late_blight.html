Tomato Late Blight

Tomato late blight, caused by the pathogen Phytophthora infestans, is a destructive disease affecting tomato plants. It leads to large, dark, water-soaked lesions on leaves, stems, and fruits, often resulting in complete crop loss.

Symptoms include irregularly shaped brown or black spots that rapidly enlarge. Leaves may appear greasy and wilt, leading to defoliation. Infected fruits develop dark, firm lesions that spread quickly.

The disease spreads through spores in moist and cool conditions. It can survive in infected plant debris, making it difficult to control.

Effective management includes removing infected plants, improving air circulation, and using fungicides.

Medicines

Mancozeb: A protective fungicide commonly used to prevent and control late blight in tomatoes.

Chlorothalonil: A broad-spectrum fungicide effective in controlling fungal infections in tomatoes.

Azoxystrobin: A systemic fungicide providing long-lasting protection against late blight.

Copper Oxychloride: A contact fungicide widely used to control late blight in tomatoes.
