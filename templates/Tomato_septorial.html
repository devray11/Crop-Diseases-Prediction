Tomato Septoria

Tomato septoria, caused by the fungus Septoria lycopersici, is a common disease affecting tomato plants. It leads to numerous small, dark-edged spots on leaves, significantly reducing plant vigor and fruit yield.

Symptoms include circular brown lesions with gray centers, primarily on lower leaves. These spots eventually cause leaf yellowing and drop, weakening the plant and affecting fruit production.

The disease spreads through splashing water, wind, and contaminated soil. Warm, humid conditions favor its development, making control challenging.

Effective management includes crop rotation, proper plant spacing, and the use of fungicides.

Medicines

Mancozeb: A protective fungicide commonly used to prevent and control septoria leaf spot in tomatoes.

Chlorothalonil: A broad-spectrum fungicide effective in controlling fungal infections in tomatoes.

Azoxystrobin: A systemic fungicide providing long-lasting protection against septoria leaf spot.

Copper Oxychloride: A contact fungicide widely used to control septoria leaf spot in tomatoes.
